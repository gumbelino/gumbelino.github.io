Participatory Budgeting Evanston | Northwestern University | 2021-2024 #civic education
How might we increase participation and representation in government programs? In partnership with the City of Evanston, I designed and implemented the outreach research program that increased participation from 800 to 6,500+ participants and brought 2,000+ new faces to a pilot participatory budgeting program to allocate $3.5M in COVID-relief funds.

Impacts:
  • Pioneered research strategy that increased participation from 1% to 8.4% of local population (78K residents) in a participatory budgeting program through iterative testing of outreach strategies
  • Designed research-driven educational materials used to train 100+ volunteers
  • Increased representation to match city's demographics by conducting large-scale survey (8K+ participants) and strategic outreach to underrepresented communities
  • Developed personas & journey maps by uncovering residents' needs, motivations, and behavior through 30+ interviews
  • Synthesized and communicated research findings and opportunities for technology improvement to local government stakeholders and academic communities
Methods: case study, surveys, document analysis, observations

Tools: Atlas.ti, CRMs (i.e., Brevo, NationBuilder, HubSpot)

Team: Morgan Wu, Kristine Lu, Matthew Easterday

AI in Politics | Centre for Democracy Studies Aarau | 2025-present #AI in politics
What is the role of large-language models (LLMs) in political decision-making? I'm running online experiments with LLMs to answer these questions. I am also developing an R package to help other researchers analyze political decisions data more effectively and without prior programming experience.

Impacts:
  • Developed a benchmark for large language models (LLMs) to help technology designers and governments decide when and how to adopt LLMs in deliberation
  • Building a scalable, user-centric R tool for efficient analysis of political survey data using a variety of statistical tests (e.g., t-tests, ANOVA, permutation tests)
Methods: surveys, descriptive and inferential statistical analysis (e.g., t-tests, ANOVA, permutation tests)

Tools: R, Shiny, Python, and a variety of LLM APIs (e.g., OpenAI, xAI)

Team: Francesco Veri (PM)
